Dodge Tomahawk - 2004
The Dodge Tomahawk is a concept motorcycle unveiled in 2003, known for its futuristic design and extreme performance. It features an 8.3-liter V10 engine from the Dodge Viper, producing around 500 horsepower. With a unique four-wheel design (two front and two rear wheels), it was engineered more as a showpiece than a street-legal vehicle. The Tomahawk is capable of reaching theoretical speeds over 300 mph, making it one of the most powerful and visually striking motorcycles ever created.
Electrom LEV - 2019
The Electrom LEV (Light Electric Vehicle) Motorcycle is a streamlined, pedal-assisted electric motorcycle designed for high-efficiency urban and long-distance travel. Featuring a recumbent riding position, enclosed bodywork, and a robust electric drivetrain, it combines the speed and power of a motorcycle with the efficiency and comfort of an e-bike. With a top speed of around 100 km/h (62 mph) and a range exceeding 200 km (124 miles), the Electrom LEV offers a unique alternative to traditional motorcycles and electric bicycles.
Quasar - 2005
The Quasar is a rare British motorcycle from the 1970s–80s, known for its unique feet-forward riding position and semi-enclosed design. Powered by a Reliant 850cc engine, it offered car-like comfort, weather protection, and strong performance, with only 21 units ever made. Powered by an 850cc water-cooled four-cylinder engine sourced from the Reliant Robin, the Quasar delivers approximately 41 horsepower, enabling cruising speeds between 90–100 mph (145–160 km/h) and potential top speeds exceeding 100 mph under favourable conditions.
Yamaha Niken - 2018
​The Yamaha Niken is a pioneering three-wheeled motorcycle that redefines cornering stability and rider confidence. Introduced in 2018, it features Yamaha’s Leaning Multi-Wheel (LMW) technology, allowing the two front wheels to tilt in unison, delivering exceptional grip and control, especially in challenging riding conditions. Powered by an 847cc liquid-cooled inline three-cylinder engine, the Niken produces 115 horsepower at 10,000 rpm and 87.5 Nm of torque at 8,500 rpm . Its advanced chassis includes dual 15-inch front wheels with independent suspension, enabling a lean angle of up to 45 degrees, and a 17-inch rear wheel. The bike comes equipped with a suite of rider aids, including ABS, traction control, cruise control, and a quick-shift system
Rokon 360 - 1971
​The Rokon 360 is a rugged, American-made, two-wheel-drive motorcycle designed for extreme off-road conditions. Equipped with a 208cc Kohler four-stroke engine producing 7 HP, it features a full-time front and rear wheel drive system, enabling it to climb 60% grades and ford water up to 24 inches deep . Its hollow aluminum drum wheels can store up to 2.5 gallons of fuel or water each, and when empty, provide enough buoyancy for the bike to float. With a dry weight of 218 lbs and a payload capacity of 600 lbs, the Rokon 360 is built for durability and versatility. It offers a top speed of approximately 59.55 km/h and includes features like an automatic torque converter, three-speed transmission, and hydraulic disc brakes
Alligator - 2002
The Alligator Motorcycle is a custom, high-performance bike designed for both off-road and on-road use. Powered by a 600cc to 1200cc V-twin or parallel-twin engine, it features a rugged steel or aluminum frame, long-travel suspension, and oversized knobby tires for superior traction. The bike includes hydraulic disc brakes, a large-capacity fuel tank (12-20 liters), and modern electronics like GPS and electronic fuel injection (EFI). With a bold, reptile-inspired design, it combines durability, style, and power, making it ideal for adventurous riders seeking performance on diverse terrains.
Honda e-NSR - 2001
The Honda e-NSR is a concept electric motorcycle unveiled at the 2001 Tokyo Motor Show. Designed to mirror the sporty NSR series, it features an electric powertrain, a compact and aerodynamic frame, and advanced suspension technology. The e-NSR's performance focused on lightweight handling and efficient energy use, with a top speed optimized for city and track use. Although it was never mass-produced, the e-NSR served as an early example of Honda's potential in electric motorcycles, showcasing the integration of high-performance design and electric mobility.
Honda Riding Assist - 2017
Honda Riding Assist is an advanced self-balancing technology designed for motorcycles, using robotics-derived systems rather than traditional gyroscopes. The system utilizes a steer-by-wire mechanism to electronically control the angle of the front fork and subtly adjust the front wheel’s direction, maintaining balance without rider input. This system is based on technologies from Honda’s UNI-CUB mobility device and ASIMO robot. The Riding Assist technology allows the motorcycle to stay upright at low speeds and when stationary. It also features the ability to autonomously follow the rider at walking speed. This reduces the risk of tipping over, making it ideal for novice riders, those with shorter stature, or when navigating tight spaces.
Honda Motocompo / BOXX - 1981
The Honda Motocompo is a compact and unique folding scooter introduced in 1981. Designed to fit in the trunk of Honda's City car, it became an innovative solution for urban mobility. Powered by a 49cc two-stroke engine, it could reach a top speed of around 30 mph (48 km/h). Its small size, lightweight frame, and folding design made it a perfect companion for city dwellers looking for an easy and efficient way to navigate through traffic. The Motocompo was a quirky, fun addition to Honda's lineup, blending practicality with an unmistakable retro charm.
Honda ATC 250r - 1981
The Honda ATC 250R is a three-wheeled all-terrain vehicle (ATV) produced from 1981 to 1986, specifically designed for high-performance off-road riding. It is powered by a 246cc, air-cooled, two-stroke single-cylinder engine, delivering strong performance with a 6-speed manual transmission and a chain final drive. The engine utilizes a reed valve system for improved fuel efficiency and power delivery. The ATC 250R features a single-shock rear suspension with a long-travel, independent front suspension (leading-axle design), allowing it to handle rough terrains with better stability and comfort. This model is known for its agile handling, thanks to a combination of a short wheelbase, low center of gravity, and strong performance.
Honda Pokemon Koraidon - 2025
The Honda Koraidon is a life-sized concept motorcycle inspired by the legendary Pokémon Koraidon from *Pokémon Scarlet*. Standing 2.5 meters tall and weighing 303 kg, it features cutting-edge technology, including Honda’s Riding Assist system, which allows it to balance itself and transition between two modes: a "Sprinting Build" that mimics Koraidon’s running posture and a "Walking Build" with four-legged movement. The bike is also equipped with animated eyes, facial expressions, and limb movements that change with speed, giving it a lifelike appearance. The Honda Koraidon showcases Honda’s innovative approach to merging mobility with Pokémon fandom, making its debut in 2025.
BMW C1 - 2000
The BMW C1 is a unique scooter-style motorcycle produced by BMW from 2000 to 2003. It features a fully enclosed cabin with a built-in safety roll cage, seatbelts, and a helmet, offering protection similar to that of a car. This innovative design aimed to provide a safer and more comfortable riding experience in urban environments. Powered by a 125cc or 200cc engine, the C1 was designed for city commuting, offering excellent fuel efficiency and easy handling. Although it was ahead of its time, the C1 was discontinued due to limited market appeal and high production costs.
Peraves Mono Tracer - 2008
The Peraves MonoTracer is a Swiss-designed cabin motorcycle that combines the excitement of a motorcycle with the safety and comfort of a car. Powered by a 1,200cc BMW engine, it offers impressive performance, reaching speeds over 250 km/h. The enclosed body provides protection from the elements, while features like ABS, stability control, and deployable stabilizer wheels enhance safety. Inside, it boasts RECARO seats, air conditioning, and a high-quality sound system. With its unique design and limited production, the MonoTracer is an exclusive, high-performance vehicle.
Mtt 420 rr - 2000
The MTT 450RR is a high-performance, turbine-powered motorcycle with a Rolls-Royce Allison 250 engine, delivering 420 horsepower and a top speed over 440 km/h. It features a lightweight carbon fiber frame, Öhlins suspension, and Dymag carbon wheels for exceptional handling. With a 34-liter fuel tank and a 2-speed automatic transmission, this bike blends cutting-edge technology with extreme speed, making it one of the fastest production motorcycles.
Royal Enfield Taurus - 1908
​The Royal Enfield Taurus, introduced in 1993, holds the distinction of being India's first and only mass-produced diesel motorcycle. Powered by a 325cc air-cooled single-cylinder diesel engine, it produced 6.5 horsepower and 15 Nm of torque, mated to a 4-speed manual gearbox . Weighing 196 kg, it boasted impressive fuel efficiency, achieving around 80–90 km/l . However, its high vibrations, excessive noise, and black smoke emissions led to its discontinuation in 2000, as it couldn't meet evolving emission standards . Despite its brief production run, the Taurus remains a unique chapter in Royal Enfield's history.
Ural Motorcycle - 1950
Ural motorcycles are rugged, all-terrain bikes with a distinctive sidecar configuration, powered by a 749cc or 806cc air-cooled, 2-cylinder, 4-stroke engine producing around 41-45 horsepower. These motorcycles feature a 4-speed manual transmission with a reverse gear and are available in both 2WD and 1WD models, with the 2WD system engaging the sidecar wheel for enhanced traction on rough terrains. Their steel frame and independent suspension—telescopic forks at the front and hydraulic shock absorbers at the rear—offer durability and off-road capability. Ural bikes are equipped with drum brakes, with disc brake options available for improved stopping power.
